                                                                                          Ring messages

                                                                                          Ring, which stands for rider in-app messaging, is our primary in-app messaging channel for communicating with customers. 

                                                                                          Ring is used by most teams at Uber to communicate with riders, Uber Eats users, and other customers. It’s used most commonly for marketing and sales messages, but it can also be used for essential product, legal, and safety messages.


                                                                                          TL;DR

                                                                                          • Ring headers should entice a user to take action, since they are the only element that shows on small devices

                                                                                          • Focus CTAs on tangible, specific action; be clear over clever

                                                                                          • Character count (standard—partial screen)

                                                                                            • Header: 25

                                                                                            • Body: 52 

                                                                                            • CTA: 15

                                                                                          • Character count (interstitial—whole screen)

                                                                                            • Header: 25

                                                                                            • Body: 60 (no bullets) or 30 (each bullet, up to 3)

                                                                                            • CTA: 15

                                                                                          • Get Ring training in this video and join #rider-support-ring on Slack for additional questions and advice

                                                                                          Anatomy of a Ring message

                                                                                          There are 2 types of Ring messages: standard messages, which take up a portion of the screen, and interstitial messages, which take the whole screen. They can include these elements:

                                                                                          • Key image 

                                                                                          • Header

                                                                                          • Body

                                                                                          • CTA


                                                                                          Standard Ring message

                                                                                          To accommodate all device sizes, Standard Ring messages can be displayed in 3 sizes:

                                                                                          • Small: header only

                                                                                          • Medium: header + CTA

                                                                                          • Large: header + body + CTA

                                                                                          Evergreen Ring message

                                                                                          Uber keeps an inventory of evergreen Ring messages to use when there are no other Ring messages to show. They’re updated regularly (by marketing) as new products and promotions become available. Go to the evergreen Ring playbook for information.


                                                                                          Writing guidelines

                                                                                          Each Ring message should answer one or more of these questions for the user: 

                                                                                          • What are we telling the user about?

                                                                                          • Why should the user care?

                                                                                          • How does the user take action?

                                                                                          Header

                                                                                          • To make Ring messages effective for devices of all sizes, headers have to tell the whole story — or at least enough of the story to entice a user to take action.

                                                                                          • Headers can be questions, calls to action, or simple statements.
